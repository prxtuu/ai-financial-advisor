Educational Modules

Learn the mechanics of different ways to generate wealth before you invest.

🏢

Property Investment

Real estate involves buying land or buildings to earn rental income or sell for a profit.

Pros: Tangible asset, potential for regular rental income, historical appreciation.

Cons: Highly illiquid (hard to sell fast), requires large capital/loans, ongoing maintenance costs.

Risk Level: Medium to High (depending on leverage).

🔄

Bank SWP & SIP

Systematic Investment (SIP) and Withdrawal Plans (SWP) automate your cash flow.

SIP: Regularly investing a fixed amount to average out market volatility.

SWP: Regularly withdrawing a fixed amount from a lump sum investment (like a pension).

Risk Level: Varies heavily based on the underlying mutual fund chosen.

📈

Company Shares & ETFs

Buying a piece of a company (Shares) or a basket of many companies (Exchange Traded Funds).

Shares: High risk, high reward. If the company fails, you lose money.

ETFs: Lower risk than individual shares because it diversifies across an entire index (e.g., top 50 companies).

Risk Level: Medium (ETFs) to Very High (Individual small-cap shares).

📦

Wholesale Business

Buying goods in bulk at a discount to sell to retailers or directly to consumers.

Pros: Total control over the business, potentially massive profit margins.

Cons: Requires inventory management, logistics, intense market research, and high active effort.

Risk Level: Very High (requires specialized knowledge to succeed).
